Like many of you, my father was never around from an early age. You see my parents split when I was young, and between my father was either living far away or spending time in prison, I was all alone with just mum and my 4 sisters. While my mother did an amazing job under the circumstances, it often meant that I felt lost and had to learn many things the hard way. Almost all of you dads out there, like me, have probably had those moments where we wonder if we are enough, or even thoughts as dark as wondering if they would be better off without us. Let me just assure you right now, for better or worse, kids need their dad.

Raising kids from a fathers perspective these days is... complicated. Finding the balance between making your kids tough enough to cope with the real world, and being a loving nurturing parent is like drink driving on uni-cycle while juggling hot coal. In the end we are all going to get things wrong, it's human nature, but one thing I'm certain of is that as long as you are showing up and they know they are loved, you're on the right path.

I had to learn some harsh lessons over the years, but this just means my son won't have to. Here are 10 things I wish my father had told me:
